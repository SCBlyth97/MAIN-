2025 – Present

Teaching Associate & Project Coordinator

Chai Wan  ·  Hong Kong Institute of Vocational Education (IVE)

I currently work as a Project Coordinator and Teaching Associate, delivering tertiary-level English education at a vocational institution in Hong Kong. My role combines teaching, educational design, and project coordination, with a focus on creating engaging and practical learning experiences for students.

A significant part of my work involves developing educational materials and learning content. Each month I create a range of resources including presentations, worksheets, posters, and other teaching materials that support students in building their English communication skills. Alongside this, I design and run interactive workshops that teach English through practical, hands-on activities rather than traditional grammar-based lessons.

These workshops are designed to make language learning enjoyable and accessible. Activities have included cooking sessions, darts games, and creative tasks such as making balloon animals. The goal is to provide students with opportunities to practise vocabulary and communication skills in relaxed, real-world contexts. Over time, I have refined these workshops to focus on the most useful language and communication skills for students, while still keeping the sessions engaging enough that students are motivated to participate.

I also work extensively on visual learning materials and content design. One of my larger projects involved taking over 1,300 pages of course documentation and transforming it into a concise 30-page series of infographics for a module focused on pitching and public speaking. These materials were designed in a fun, comic-style format to make complex information easier for students to understand and remember. An example of one of these infographics can be seen below.

In addition to workshops and material development, I provide daily tutoring support for students who want additional help with their English. For a small group of motivated students, I also offer voluntary support outside of my core duties to teach English literacy from the very beginning. Using the Ruth Miskin Read Write Inc. phonics approach, I teach students the foundational sounds, spelling patterns, and reading strategies needed to build literacy from the ground up.

This has been one of the most meaningful aspects of my work. Over the course of a year, I was able to help several students who previously could not read English develop the ability to read unfamiliar words independently. Although this work falls outside my formal job responsibilities, helping students achieve that level of progress has been one of the achievements I am most proud of in my teaching career.

I also regularly use design tools such as Canva alongside AI-assisted tools to create engaging learning materials, posters, and visual resources that support student learning.

As part of my Teaching Associate responsibilities, I frequently work directly within classrooms alongside other instructors. In these sessions, I deliver specialised workshops on topics such as non-verbal communication, polite disagreement in business settings, presentation skills, managing nerves during public speaking, and professional communication.

I also support teaching staff by helping students prepare for their spoken and written assessments. This includes listening to practice presentations, reviewing scripts, and providing constructive feedback to help students refine their communication skills before their final assignments.
