07/2023 – 03/2025

English Teacher

Mei Foo, Hong Kong  ·  Babington Education Centre

After graduating from university in December 2022, I spent several months searching for opportunities in education. When I was unable to secure a position locally, I made the decision to take a significant step: in July 2023 I moved to Hong Kong to pursue teaching opportunities. Relocating to a new country on short notice was a challenging experience, but it ultimately became one of the most rewarding decisions I have made in my career.

I joined Babington Education Centre in Mei Foo, where I worked as an English teacher. My role involved teaching both the Read Write Inc. Phonics programme developed by Ruth Miskin, as well as the centre's proprietary English Ladder curriculum.

The phonics-based approach focused on developing strong reading and pronunciation skills through structured sound learning, while the English Ladder programme placed greater emphasis on grammar, comprehension, and structured language development. Teaching both systems gave me a much deeper understanding of how English works as a language. Coming from an academic background in biology and geography, I had to learn a great deal about English instruction on the job. I spent considerable time researching teaching methods and self-studying linguistic and pedagogical concepts so that I could deliver lessons effectively and confidently.

During my time at the centre, I noticed that some internal processes were not fully standardised. To improve efficiency and reduce errors, I created a centralised organisational system using Notion, which documented procedures, teaching resources, and operational guidelines. This system was shared with the wider team, and several colleagues adopted it as a way to streamline their work and improve consistency across the organisation.

The early months in the role were challenging as I adapted to a new work culture and clarified expectations within the workplace. However, over time I built a strong professional relationship with my manager and colleagues. When my manager went on maternity leave for three months, I was entrusted with a high level of responsibility for the day-to-day operation of my classes and the coordination of student and parent communication. During this period, a colleague and I successfully maintained the smooth running of the centre's teaching schedule and ensured that students continued to receive consistent support and instruction.

Another important area of personal growth during this role was learning to work effectively with very young learners, particularly kindergarten students. Early in my time at the centre I was encouraged to improve my approach with this age group. Over the course of the year, I focused on developing greater patience, adapting my teaching style, and learning how to engage younger students more effectively. By the end of my time there, I had developed strong confidence working with young children and found it to be one of the most rewarding aspects of teaching.

Overall, my time at Babington was an important period of professional development. It strengthened my teaching skills, deepened my understanding of English education, and helped me grow both personally and professionally while adapting to life and work in a new country.
