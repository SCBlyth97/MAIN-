Who I am & where I'm going

Assistant lecturer, project coordinator, and deep thinker. Based in Hong Kong. Working toward something real.

I'm Stanley Blyth — an assistant lecturer, project coordinator, and PGCE-qualified teacher currently based in Hong Kong.

My background is originally in environmental management, but I've spent the last several years moving between education, project coordination, and an ongoing curiosity about how systems — biological, social, technical — actually work. I have a habit of going three layers deeper than necessary into anything and everything that interests me. This site is where that ends up.

Teaching gave me a structured way of thinking about how people learn and how to explain complex things clearly. I still use that every day, even when I'm not in a classroom. The ability to break something down until it makes sense, then rebuild it, shapes most of how I approach problems.

Hong Kong is where I live and work. It's fast, dense, and genuinely interesting. I workout, I read, I think about the future, and I spend more time than most people would consider reasonable going deep on things that catch my attention.

I grew up in South Africa, and that shapes everything about how I move through the world. If you understand what South Africa is like, you'll know it's the kind of place that can get under your skin. It teaches you community in a way that is not abstract: you look out for your neighbours because they look out for you and you really do need each other — it's "Ubuntu". You learn to get along with people who are nothing like you, and you find that is not a compromise, it is actually the good part. There is a warmth and a directness to that culture that I carry with me everywhere I go.

It also teaches you to be practical in a very specific way. Resources are limited, supply chains are unreliable, and waiting for someone else to solve your problem is rarely an option. You learn to work with what you have, improvise when you have to, and get on with it regardless. That instinct — to assess, adapt, and move forward — is probably the most useful thing I brought into my professional life.

Working in Hong Kong has been an interesting counterbalance. Where South Africa taught me to think on my feet, Hong Kong taught me to work within systems. Institutions here run on policy, procedure, and documentation, and I have learned to operate effectively inside that structure. Knowing when to follow the rulebook precisely and when to think independently is what actually makes you effective. South Africa gave me one half. Hong Kong gave me the other.

The long-term goal is a farm — land and animals and a vegetable patch, a kind of deliberate, intentional life built around tangible work and real cycles. That's not imminent. But it's the direction, and having a clear direction matters.

If my word isn't enough, here is what a previous employer had to say:

His positive attitude and willingness to assist others create a harmonious work environment, fostering a sense of camaraderie among staff. In summary, I wholeheartedly recommend Stanley Blyth for any position or opportunity that he seeks."
