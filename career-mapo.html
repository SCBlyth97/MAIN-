2023

Account Manager — Software Start-Up

South Africa  ·  Mapos Solutions

After completing my university degree at the end of 2022, I briefly worked with Mapos Solutions, a startup focused on automotive data analysis and cost modelling.

The company had developed a specialised system that compiled electronic parts catalogue data from major vehicle manufacturers operating in South Africa. This allowed the organisation to track the cost and structure of vehicle components in extraordinary detail. The system contained information on billions of individual automotive parts, enabling the company to analyse the full cost structure of vehicles on the South African market.

I initially joined the company in a data entry and data processing role, where my responsibility was to extract information from manufacturer parts catalogues and input new or updated components into the company's internal database. While the work was repetitive, it gave me an in-depth understanding of how the data system functioned.

During this process, I identified a way to streamline the workflow by using internal system codes to quickly check whether parts data already existed or had become outdated. By adapting the process, I was able to reduce what would normally require around 12,000 manual entries to roughly 68 targeted inputs, dramatically improving efficiency. This allowed me to complete approximately a month's worth of work within a week.

Because of this efficiency, I was later given the opportunity to assist with automotive research and report writing. The company produced Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) reports, which analysed the full cost structure of vehicles by combining manufacturer parts data with operational and maintenance costs. These reports helped organisations better understand long-term vehicle ownership costs and, in some cases, provided insights that manufacturers could use to improve vehicle design.

The company's database and analytical system represented a highly specialised dataset, giving the organisation a unique advantage in the automotive analytics market. However, the broader economic climate presented challenges. The business was operating in the period immediately following the difficult financial conditions of 2022, and many companies were hesitant to adopt new analytical software during that time.

During a strategy discussion with my manager, I proposed introducing a limited-time demo version of the software, allowing potential clients to test the platform for several months before committing to a full licence. This approach significantly improved adoption, and more than 70% of companies that used the demo ultimately signed on as paying customers. Although the software was a significant investment for clients, the financial insights it provided often resulted in substantial savings and strategic value for organisations using it.

Working at a startup also meant operating with very limited resources. Instead of relying on expensive enterprise tools, we made extensive use of open-source software and creative problem-solving to build systems and complete projects. This environment taught me how to accomplish complex tasks efficiently with minimal resources, while continuously adapting processes as the company grew.

The role required a high degree of independence. Tasks that would typically involve several specialists in a larger company were often handled by a single person, which meant learning quickly and developing new technical skills on the job. During this time I also began experimenting with AI-assisted tools to help analyse data, generate drafts of reports, and streamline research tasks. This experience introduced me to practical applications of AI in workflow optimisation and was one of the moments that sparked my long-term interest in technology and digital tools.

Although my time at Mapos Solutions was relatively short, it was an incredibly engaging and formative experience. The startup environment fostered a strong sense of ownership and problem-solving, and it was one of the first times in my career where I felt that the work I was doing had a direct and visible impact on the organisation.

I eventually left the company to relocate to Hong Kong and pursue my career in education. I left on very positive terms, and the experience remains one of the most enjoyable and influential roles I have had, particularly in shaping my interest in startups, technology, and innovative problem-solving.
