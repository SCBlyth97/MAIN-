The full story: MAPO Software Solutions

Two wins, one promotion, and everything that needed doing alongside them.

Context Win 01 · Automation Win 02 · Strategy Supporting work

February 2023 · South Africa

Account Manager, early stage

MAPO Software Solutions

I joined MAPO as one of the first hires at an automotive software startup. The title was Account Manager. The job description was a loose framework. What it actually meant was sales, client management, internal operations, and whatever else needed doing on a given day.

MAPO built fleet management and Total Cost of Ownership software for automotive dealers and OEMs. The client base was dealership groups across South Africa. The OEM relationships spanned a significant range of manufacturers: Ford, Mercedes, the full Stellantis group, Jaguar Land Rover, Volkswagen, Renault, and others. The product worked. The company was small and moving fast.

This page covers what I actually worked on: two projects that measurably improved the business, and the operational work that ran alongside them.

Win 01 · Process Automation

Four people. One month. Then one file.

Total Cost of Ownership reporting · OEM data processing

By the end of my first month at the company, the process that had required four staff members working for a full month had been replaced by a single person uploading a file.

I automated myself out of my original function. My manager promoted me.

The problem

Building Total Cost of Ownership reports required approximately 12,000 manual data inputs per cycle. The company worked with a wide range of OEMs — Ford, Mercedes, the Stellantis group, Jaguar Land Rover, Volkswagen, Renault, and others — each running a different file format and reporting structure. The core of the manual work was cross-referencing: incoming files had to be compared against existing records to identify which parts hadn't been replaced yet, extracting only the new data forward. Multiplied across manufacturers and thousands of vehicles, that comparison was where most of the 12,000 inputs came from. Four people. About a month. Every cycle.

The process worked. It was also slow, error-prone, and entirely manual from end to end.

What changed

There were two problems to solve. The first was the cross-referencing. I built a process that automated the comparison step: incoming files were compared against existing records automatically, extracting only unreplaced parts and producing a clean output. That removed the bulk of the manual work.

The second was the format problem. Every OEM structured their files differently, which meant each one required manual interpretation before anything could be entered into the system. I built the software to read incoming files regardless of how they were laid out and auto-populate the system from whatever it received — no manual reformatting required.

Two manual steps remained: receive the files, upload the file. Everything in between was handled automatically. Four people working for a month became one person working for minutes.

In building this, I had automated myself out of my original function. That got me promoted.

Summary

  • Automated the cross-reference step: incoming OEM files are compared automatically and only unreplaced parts are extracted
  • Built format-agnostic file reading compatible with every OEM's reporting structure — Ford, Mercedes, Stellantis, Jaguar Land Rover, Volkswagen, Renault, and others
  • Reduced approximately 12,000 manual data inputs to two steps: receive the files, upload the file
  • Process time reduced from roughly one month (four people) to minutes (one person)
  • Built and implemented within the first month at the company
  • Resulted in a promotion to Account Manager

Win 02 · Product Strategy

The 90-day trial. 70% conversion.

Sales pipeline · Demo strategy · Client acquisition

Approximately 70% of clients who entered the demo period converted to paid subscriptions.

After the promotion, I was managing the sales pipeline. It was moving slowly. The hesitation from prospective clients was not about the product. South Africa's automotive sector was still recalibrating post-COVID, and dealership managers were cautious about new software commitments, particularly where they hadn't seen the system work against their own data in their own context.

A standard pitch was not going to close that gap. What they needed was experience with the product before they committed to it.

The solution

I proposed a 90-day demo mode. Full product access, using the client's own data, for 90 days. After that, access expired unless they converted. The expiry was fixed.

The logic drew on how the same problem gets solved outside automotive software. Platforms like Steam built large user bases through free access that converted once users had meaningful experience with the product. SaaS free-tier models work on the same principle: remove the friction at first contact, let the product demonstrate its value, and give the relationship time to develop before asking for a financial commitment. The key addition was the hard cutoff. Open-ended free access removes the conversion pressure. A defined deadline restores it.

For clients where price was the stated barrier, I negotiated value-for-access arrangements: a case study, a referral, a co-marketing contribution. Something useful to the company that didn't require cash up front.

The product closes the deal. The demo just has to get them there.

Results

The 70% conversion rate held across the demo cohort.

One dealership manager chose not to convert at the end of his 90 days. I went above him to the group owner. The owner signed all nine dealerships in the group.

I also brokered a direct partnership between two existing MAPO clients. The arrangement moved over 100 vehicles between their lots and generated referrals back to MAPO from both sides.

Summary

  • Approximately 70% demo-to-paid conversion rate across the cohort
  • 90-day hard-expiry trial model, designed and implemented from scratch
  • Negotiated value-for-access deals for price-sensitive clients
  • Converted a full nine-dealership group after a single manager declined
  • Brokered a client-to-client partnership that moved 100+ vehicles and produced referrals back to MAPO

Everything else that needed doing
